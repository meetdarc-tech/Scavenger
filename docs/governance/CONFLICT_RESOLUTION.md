# Conflict Resolution Process

This document describes how technical and interpersonal conflicts are handled in the Scavngr project.

---

## Scope

This process covers:

- **Technical disputes** — disagreements about design decisions, code direction, or feature priority.
- **Interpersonal conflicts** — friction between contributors or maintainers.
- **Code of Conduct violations** — behaviour that violates the Contributor Covenant.

---

## Technical Disputes

### Step 1 — Direct discussion

The parties should first attempt to resolve the dispute directly through a respectful, evidence-based discussion in the relevant GitHub issue or PR. Each side should:

- State their position clearly.
- Acknowledge the other side's valid points.
- Propose a concrete resolution or compromise.

### Step 2 — Maintainer mediation

If direct discussion does not resolve the dispute after 7 days, either party may request maintainer mediation by adding the `conflict-resolution` label to the issue.

An uninvolved maintainer will review the thread, ask clarifying questions, and make a recommendation within 7 days.

### Step 3 — Maintainer vote

If mediation does not resolve the dispute, any maintainer may call a vote following the Type 2 or Type 3 decision process (see [Decision-Making Framework](decision-making.md)).

The outcome of the vote is binding.

---

## Interpersonal Conflicts

If a contributor feels they have been treated disrespectfully (without a Code of Conduct violation), they should:

1. If comfortable, address the person directly with a specific, private message.
2. If not comfortable, contact any maintainer privately (contact details in `SECURITY.md`).
3. The contacted maintainer will facilitate a resolution confidentially.

All parties are expected to act in good faith. If the conflict escalates or is unresolved after two maintainer-mediation attempts, it will be escalated to a full maintainer vote on the appropriate course of action (including removal of a contributor from the project if necessary).

---

## Code of Conduct Violations

Violations of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/) should be reported to the maintainers via the contact listed in `SECURITY.md`.

### Enforcement ladder

| Severity | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Minor first offence | Private written warning |
| Repeated minor offence or single moderate offence | Temporary ban (14–30 days) |
| Severe offence | Permanent ban |

All enforcement decisions are made by maintainer consensus (minimum 2 maintainers) and are documented in a private log. The reporter will receive a response within 72 hours.

---

## Transparency

- Technical dispute resolutions are documented on the relevant GitHub issue.
- CoC enforcement outcomes are not publicly disclosed to protect reporter and respondent privacy.
- Aggregate CoC statistics (number of reports, number of enforcement actions, no personal details) are included in the quarterly transparency report.

---

## No Retaliation

Contributors who report conflicts or Code of Conduct violations in good faith are protected from retaliation. Any retaliatory behaviour is itself a CoC violation and will be treated accordingly.
